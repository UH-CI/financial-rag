Sir:
Your Committee on
Ways and Means, to which was referred H.B. No. 1483, H.D. 1,
S.D. 1, entitled:
"A BILL FOR AN ACT RELATING TO FIREWORKS,"
begs leave to report as follows:
The
purpose and intent of this measure is to strengthen state fireworks laws.
Specifically,
the measure:
(1)
Amends various definitions relating to
fireworks;
(2)
Increases certain penalties for fireworks
offenses;
(3)
Establishes various criminal offenses and
penalties related to fireworks or articles pyrotechnic;
(4)
Establishes processes relating to the
adjudication of fireworks infractions; and
(5)
Appropriates moneys.
Your Committee
received written comments in support of this measure from the Department of the
Attorney General; Honolulu Police Department; Hawaii County Police Department;
Department of the Prosecuting Attorney, County of Maui; Hawaiian Humane
Society; and four individuals.
Your Committee
received written comments in opposition to this measure from Hawaii Explosives and
Pyrotechnics and one individual.
Your Committee
recognizes the need to reform the State's approach to enforcing fireworks
laws.
Your Committee believes that this
measure appropriately addresses shortcomings in the current regulatory
framework and establishes a sensible penalty structure that imposes just
consequences based on the severity of violations.
As affirmed by the
record of votes of the members of your Committee on Ways and Means that is
attached to this report, your Committee is in accord with the intent and
purpose of H.B. No. 1483, H.D. 1, S.D. 1, and recommends that it
pass Third Reading.
Respectfully submitted on behalf
of the members of the Committee on Ways and Means,
